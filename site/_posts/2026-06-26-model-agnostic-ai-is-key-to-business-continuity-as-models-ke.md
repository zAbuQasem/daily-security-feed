SonarSource argues that organizations should treat AI models as interchangeable contributors rather than irreplaceable authorities, requiring model-agnostic architectures with central verification layers to mitigate vendor lock-in risk. The article emphasizes that as AI models change, disappear, or degrade (citing real examples of 68–147 vulnerabilities per million lines in tested models), business continuity depends on owned governance processes, not model selection. Key controls include static analysis (SonarQube), quality gates, code review layers, and risk-based access control that allow swapping models without workflow collapse. The thesis reframes AI governance from prompt-level inputs to verifiable, repeatable development workflows anchored in security standards rather than model capability.
