A practical framework for efficient alert triage in incident response. The article recommends four key techniques: severity-based filtering (prioritize High/Critical alerts to establish context for Medium/Low), baseline comparison to distinguish true anomalies from normal operations, actions-on-objective analysis (determine if suspicious activity actually advances attacker goals), and detection-intent-focused investigation (avoid rabbit holes by focusing only on what the detection rule was designed to catch). The author synthesizes these into a procedural checklist: assess alert priority, evaluate activity frequency, determine whether it furthers attacker objectives, and define what successful attack progress looks like in that context, enabling security teams to rapidly triage high-volume alert queues.
