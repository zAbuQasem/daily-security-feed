CMMC Conditional Status allows DoD contractors and subcontractors to handle Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) on systems that have not fully implemented all CMMC Level 2 or 3 requirements, providing a 180-day grace period to achieve full compliance. To qualify, organizations must achieve at least 80% of the total CMMC requirements, implement all controls with point values of 3 or 5 (with one exception for FIPS encryption), maintain a complete System Security Plan (SSP), and document remediation plans in a Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M). Specific mandatory controls that cannot be deferred include external connection authorization for CUI data (AC.L2-3.1.20) and incident response capabilities (IR.L3-3.6.1 for Level 3). The article notes that while DFARS 252.204-7021 permits Conditional Status, some prime contractors impose stricter requirements and only accept subcontractors with Final Status, limiting the practical utility of this compliance pathway.
