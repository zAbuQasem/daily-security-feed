TrustedSec's defensive research reframes AI adoption risk as a controls velocity problem rather than a new security paradigm. The core finding: employees deploy AI copilots, IDE plugins, and MCP agents faster than procurement and policy can govern them, compressing the cadence of traditional security reviews and compliance cycles. The solution maps existing pillars (IAM, DLP, SDLC, monitoring, incident response) directly onto AI systems, but rescoped: treat agents as principals with least-privilege scoped credentials, redact sensitive data at the prompt/response boundary before exfil, and maintain three-dimensional AI inventory (systems, non-human identities, data flows). This governance-first approach aligns with NIST AI RMF and OWASP LLM Top 10, providing actionable frameworks for organizations already dealing with shadow AI adoption.
