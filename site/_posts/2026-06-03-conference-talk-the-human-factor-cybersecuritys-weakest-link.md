A conference talk reframing the role of human cognition in cybersecurity defense from liability to adaptive asset. The speakers argue that human pattern recognition, contextual anomaly detection, and adaptive decision-making provide irreplaceable strengths that complement automation and algorithms. The core insight is that security systems fail not because humans are inherently weak, but because organizations incorrectly frame and design around humans as problems rather than as integral socio-technical components. Effective defense requires treating cybersecurity as a system design problem that enables good human judgment while mitigating cognitive vulnerabilities like alert fatigue, routine blindness, and cognitive biases—not through training alone, but through organizational alignment and tool support.
