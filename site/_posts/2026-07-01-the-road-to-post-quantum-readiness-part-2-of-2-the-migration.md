Part 2 of NVISO's practical guide to post-quantum cryptography (PQC) migration strategy, arguing that organizations cannot rely on hyperscaler platform upgrades to protect their full infrastructure. The article establishes why waiting for vendors is high-risk under harvest-now-decrypt-later (HNDL) threat models, where adversaries collect encrypted traffic and data today for decryption once quantum capabilities emerge. It proposes a risk-driven migration approach prioritizing critical exposures early (internal traffic, CI/CD signing, backup encryption, OT systems, third-party dependencies) rather than the common inventory-first model. The piece strengthens the business case for early action: avoiding premature procurement debt, improving supply chain visibility into vendor timelines, and positioning for regulatory and cyber insurance expectations. References CISA, NSA, and NIST guidance to contextualize organizational migration responsibility as a comprehensive IT/OT modernization effort, not a passive vendor update.
