A technical history of email protocols tracing the evolution from Ray Tomlinson's foundational user@host addressing model through SMTP and MIME standards. The article argues that internet email inherited fundamental security flaws—designed in an era of trust without provisions for message confidentiality or integrity—and contrasts SMTP's pragmatic simplicity with the OSI suite's X.400 Message Handling System, which added significant complexity through ASN.1 binary serialization but remained unsuccessful in displacing internet email standards. Valuable as context for understanding modern email architecture's legacy constraints and the protocol design tradeoffs between simplicity and security.
