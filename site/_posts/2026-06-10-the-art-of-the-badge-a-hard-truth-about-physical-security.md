A substantive defensive guide from Black Hills InfoSec on bypassing physical security through social engineering and fake badges. The article demonstrates how an attacker with a fabricated badge can exploit human psychology (politeness, assumption of authority, desire to avoid conflict) and tailgating to access restricted areas undetected. The technical recommendations cover badge discipline (visible wear, no piggybacking, separate authentication), distinctive visitor badge design with expiration, immediate access revocation upon employee departure, and building security culture through mock scenario testing. The core finding: organizational security depends on employees actively enforcing badge discipline as a cultural practice, not just on technical controls.
