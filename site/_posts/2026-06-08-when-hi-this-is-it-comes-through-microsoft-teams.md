Threat actors including Cloaked Ursa (APT29) and UNC6692 are increasingly using Microsoft Teams for phishing and social engineering, impersonating IT support to trick employees into approving MFA prompts and disclosing credentials. Attackers exploit Teams federation settings and unmanaged account policies to send external chat invitations that appear legitimate, leveraging typosquatted domains, internal naming conventions, or compromised partner accounts. Collaboration tool phishing now represents 42% of all phishing alerts (up from 30% in the prior four months), as users remain less cautious with chat platforms than email. Microsoft provides impersonation warnings but relies on user vigilance; defenders should disable federation with unmanaged tenants where possible, restrict external communications via Teams policies, and provide targeted awareness training on internal-tool-based social engineering tactics.
