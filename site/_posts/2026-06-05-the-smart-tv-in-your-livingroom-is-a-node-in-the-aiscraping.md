Bright Data operates the world's largest residential proxy network (400M+ IPs) by embedding an SDK into consumer apps that transforms phones and smart TVs into proxy exit nodes, allowing customers to route web-scraping traffic through residential connections to bypass datacenter IP blocks. Smart TVs are particularly exploited as proxies because they remain always-on with unlimited bandwidth, minimal user oversight, and poor consent UI (buried in TV-navigated legal text), with default monthly WiFi budgets reaching 200 GB per device. The SDK integrates across major platforms including Roku, LG, Samsung, and Vizio CTVs, gaming networks serving 250M+ homes, and messaging apps with hundreds of millions of users. This infrastructure enables large-scale AI training data harvesting at scale, with the FBI issuing formal advisories on residential proxy abuse in 2026. The analysis reveals the gap between disclosed consent and actual system behavior, highlighting a structural vulnerability in consumer IoT.
