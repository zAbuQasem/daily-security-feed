Weekly threat intelligence roundup covering major developments: law enforcement disrupted Amadey/StealC malware-as-a-service networks (Operation Endgame), seizing 326 servers and 142 domains; North Korean-linked macOS.Gaslight implant discovered with novel prompt-injection payload designed to derail LLM-based reverse-engineering analysis by injecting fabricated error messages mimicking the triage harness's own instructions; actively exploited Cisco Unified Communications Manager vulnerability (CVE-2026-20230) allows unauthenticated SSRF to arbitrary file writes and root privilege escalation via WebDialer service, with a second Cisco SD-WAN controller flaw (CVE-2026-20245) exploited pre-disclosure by uploading malicious CSV to create hidden root shell account. Gaslight represents escalation in adversarial tradecraft—targeting not just endpoints but AI-assisted analysis pipelines themselves.
