Modern attackers are compressing full attack lifecycles to 72 minutes by automating identity-based entry and rapid privilege escalation, representing 4X year-over-year acceleration. Unit 42 data shows 65% of initial access exploits credential compromise, MFA manipulation, or help-desk impersonation, followed by rapid privilege escalation and cross-platform lateral movement targeting identity, endpoint, cloud, and SaaS environments. Detection gaps stem not from missing signals but from fragmented tooling and sequential workflows that allow adversaries to achieve objectives before analysts manually correlate alerts. Unit 42 recommends re-engineering SOC operations for velocity: automated parallel enrichment, behavioral correlation across sources, predefined containment playbooks, and prioritizing attacker behavior patterns over static indicators. The shift from manual alert stitching to real-time AI-driven correlation is critical for defending against modern compressed timelines.
