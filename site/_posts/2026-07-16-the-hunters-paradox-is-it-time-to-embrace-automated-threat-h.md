An expert analysis of the 'Hunter's Paradox': human analysts cannot scale to modern data volumes and attack velocity, yet AI is vulnerable to deception and adversarial manipulation in threat data. The article reframes threat hunting from 'human-driven' to 'reasoning-driven,' arguing AI reasoning can be legitimate if properly scoped. Proposes three guardrails for automated threat hunting: tight procedural focus (apply AI to ambiguous hunts, not deterministic rules), strict guidelines (scoped access, audit trails, predefined autonomy boundaries), and graduated autonomy (advisor → assistant → operator progression). Defensive methodology with practical implications for blue teams considering AI-assisted security operations.
