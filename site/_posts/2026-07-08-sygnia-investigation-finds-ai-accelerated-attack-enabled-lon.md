A Sygnia incident investigation reveals an AI-assisted cloud attack that progressed from initial AWS access to broad enterprise compromise in 72 hours via agentic AI workflows. Rather than novel exploits, the threat actor leveraged well-known techniques but executed them at unprecedented speed and scale—chaining weaknesses across application services, CI/CD pipelines, repositories, and databases while simultaneously performing credential discovery, secrets harvesting, cloud enumeration, and deployment pipeline abuse. Parallel execution patterns (e.g., four access keys from the same source in one second) and hundreds of SQL queries across databases indicate centralized, AI-driven orchestration. The attacker-generated scripts exhibited characteristics of AI generation, with artifacts labeled as pentest and red team activities to disguise intent, demonstrating how agentic AI lowers the barrier to entry for threat actors to execute reconnaissance, tool development, and environment-specific adaptation at speeds that outpace typical defender response.
