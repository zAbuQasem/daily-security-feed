A weekly security news roundup covering three major stories. First, coordinated law enforcement disrupted crypto-fraud networks and sanctioned Iranian exchanges facilitating ransomware payments. Second, TA4922, a China-linked cybercrime syndicate, is expanding phishing campaigns globally using credential harvesting lures and DLL side-loading to deploy RATs (ValleyRAT, Atlas RAT) that exfiltrate Chrome credentials. Third, CVE-2026-0257 in Palo Alto PAN-OS GlobalProtect (CVSS 7.8) allows authentication bypass due to improper validation of authentication override cookies—attackers retrieve the public key via HTTPS, forge signed cookies, and gain unauthorized VPN access; active exploitation has been observed since May 17 and the vulnerability is now on CISA's KEV catalog.
