Unit 42's 2026 Incident Response Report analyzes AI's role in modern cyberattacks across hundreds of real engagements. Key finding: AI is a force multiplier for attackers (accelerating reconnaissance, phishing, malware development, and vulnerability discovery), but is not introducing fundamentally new attack vectors—threat actors continue relying on credential theft, phishing, known CVEs, and ransomware. The report highlights emerging AI-enabled capabilities including agentic ransomware orchestrating multi-stage extortion with reduced operational complexity, and token jacking attacks where adversaries exploit exposed cloud AI/LLM API credentials to generate millions in unauthorized compute charges or train malicious models. Strategic recommendation: treat AI-driven threats as a priority but defend using existing preventive controls; organizations must augment human defenders with AI-powered detection and response capabilities to keep pace with accelerating attack timelines.
