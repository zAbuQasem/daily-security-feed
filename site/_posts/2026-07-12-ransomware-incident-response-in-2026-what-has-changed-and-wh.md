Modern ransomware attacks have fundamentally shifted from encryption-first tactics to data exfiltration as the primary objective, with encryption applied after data is already stolen (or skipped entirely). Attackers now prioritize compromising identity systems, backup infrastructure, and virtualization planes using valid credentials rather than malware, reducing the window for detection and forcing defenders to rethink containment around identity compromise instead of host isolation. AI-powered reconnaissance and lateral movement occur within hours rather than days, breaking incident response assumptions about investigation time. Sygnia's analysis identifies five critical CISO mistakes: backup-only recovery strategies that ignore data extortion, lack of documented identity revocation procedures, reactive ransom frameworks defined mid-incident, and siloed response teams unable to coordinate legal/communications decisions in real-time.
