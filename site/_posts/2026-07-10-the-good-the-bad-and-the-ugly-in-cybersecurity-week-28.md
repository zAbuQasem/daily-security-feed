A SentinelOne threat roundup covering three major cybersecurity developments: Forg365, a new phishing-as-a-service platform targeting Microsoft 365 enterprise accounts, weaponizes AI-generated phishing lures, adversary-in-the-middle (AiTM) techniques, and OAuth 2.0 device-code authentication abuse to achieve account hijacking. The platform deploys a persistence mechanism via the ForgCookie browser extension (Edge, Chrome, Brave) that silently captures OAuth tokens and clears session cookies for continuous access. Additionally, concurrent state-sponsored intrusions from China and India targeted Pakistani law enforcement (Balochistan Police) between February 2024 and April 2026, with China-nexus operators deploying PlugX, ShadowPad, and Cobalt Strike malware, while India-nexus activity utilized Remcos backdoors for geopolitical intelligence gathering. Law enforcement operations also disrupted pro-Russian hacktivist groups CARR and Z-Pentest plus global fraud rings, arresting 5,811 suspects and seizing $293 million.
