The article proposes a quantitative framework for identity risk measurement, arguing that static privilege tiers and alert-driven triage both fail because they either ignore runtime behavior or lack cumulative context. It advocates for continuous, multi-dimensional risk scores combining static posture (privilege, credential hygiene, entitlements) and runtime signals (behavioral anomalies, threat intel, authentication context) across a single 0–100 scale. The key insight is score velocity: the rate of risk change across an identity is often the earliest signal of compromise, turning risk scoring from a reporting tool into an active defense mechanism. The model unifies all identity types—human users, service accounts, API keys, OAuth tokens, IAM roles, and AI agents—under the same measurement framework.
