CISA's new BOD 26-04 framework reframes vulnerability prioritization around four risk criteria: public exposure, full automation feasibility, complete system compromise capability, and real-world exploitation evidence. Organizations can now defer lower-risk vulnerabilities to the next system upgrade while focusing critical resources on the highest-risk subset — reducing mean time to remediation from 43+ days to a 3-day window for top-tier vulnerabilities. A real-world Federal agency deployment found only 1% of vulnerability instances qualify as critical (3-day SLA), with 60% deferred, improving resource efficiency and defense prioritization in an era of AI-accelerated vulnerability discovery.
