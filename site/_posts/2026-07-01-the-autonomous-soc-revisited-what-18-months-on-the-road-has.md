SentinelOne presents a maturity model for Autonomous SOC deployments based on 18 months of real-world implementations, showing that the primary barrier between AI-assisted operations and partial autonomy is organizational governance rather than technology. The framework requires pre-defined rules of engagement, accountability structures, and audit trails before autonomous response—mirroring SAE Level 3 automotive autonomy principles. Key insight: defenders must build institutional knowledge through assisted workflows to establish the data foundation and trust for higher autonomy, while attackers operate without such constraints, creating a structural tempo gap. Gartner data shows 40% of organizations evaluate AI SOC capabilities but only 18% deploy them, with the gap driven by governance readiness. The model accurately reflects real organizational transitions and provides practical sequencing for advancing from Level 2 (AI-assisted) to Level 3 (partial autonomy) through deliberate governance discipline.
