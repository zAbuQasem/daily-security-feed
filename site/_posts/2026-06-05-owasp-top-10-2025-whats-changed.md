The OWASP Top 10 2025 introduces two new categories reflecting evolving threat landscapes: A03 Software Supply Chain Failures (expanding from vulnerable components to encompass dependencies, build systems, package repositories, and distribution mechanisms) and A10 Mishandling of Exceptional Conditions (addressing improper error handling and resource exhaustion under edge cases). The update exemplifies a strategic shift from individual coding mistakes toward systemic risks in software design and development processes—OWASP now emphasizes that applications built with insecure architectures, inadequate access controls, and weak CI/CD pipelines cannot be secured through last-minute code scanning. A recent supply chain compromise illustrates the urgency: Microsoft's durabletask Python SDK was hijacked on PyPI to steal credentials and propagate through cloud infrastructures, reaching 400,000+ monthly downloads. Organizations must now embed security controls across the full development lifecycle, from threat modeling and secure design to build integrity and dependency scanning.
