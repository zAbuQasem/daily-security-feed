A professional services firm's application control policies successfully blocked a threat actor's attempts to deploy unauthorized remote access software, but the attacker immediately pivoted to social engineering, impersonating an IT support representative to convince an employee to upload files to attacker-controlled cloud storage. The incident demonstrates how determined adversaries adapt when technical controls prevent their primary intrusion path, shifting from technical exploitation to targeting human trust and business processes. Forensic investigation confirmed the compromise remained narrowly contained with no unauthorized system access or lateral movement; the attacker's preferred vector (software execution) was eliminated by application whitelisting, forcing them into a less effective alternative that limited overall impact. The case illustrates that layered defense—combining technical controls with operational safeguards around data movement, credential changes, and remote access requests—is essential to constrain business impact when attackers change tactics.
