Neil Madden analyzes the urgency claims around post-quantum cryptography transition following a new White House Executive Order mandating PQC adoption by 2030–2031. He documents a significant gap between hype and hardware reality: Google missed its 2029 target of 1M qubits by orders of magnitude (currently 105), IBM is at 156 vs 100k target by 2033, and quantum error correction breakthroughs are being repeatedly rehashed from prior announcements. Despite academic improvements to attack algorithms and theoretical qubit requirements, Madden argues actual quantum hardware progress has stalled, making panic-driven wholesale PQC replacement premature. He advocates instead for pragmatic cryptographic agility, hybrid migration, and architectural choices (symmetric crypto over public-key, stateful tokens over stateless JWTs) to mitigate "store now, decrypt later" threats while maintaining flexibility.
