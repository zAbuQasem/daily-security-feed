Sygnia's forensic investigation of the $1.5 billion Bybit heist reveals a sophisticated multi-stage attack by North Korea's Lazarus Group: social engineering a SafeWallet developer to deliver a malicious Python application, exploiting stolen AWS session tokens to register a fraudulent MFA device, then injecting malicious JavaScript into Safe{Wallet}'s S3-hosted frontend to intercept and manipulate transaction flows, ultimately siphoning 400,000 ETH to threat actor accounts before erasing forensic evidence. The case study highlights unique crypto infrastructure risks—assets move instantaneously once signing mechanisms are compromised, with detection windows measured in seconds rather than hours. Industry survey data shows 83% of crypto security leaders experienced cyberattacks in the past 12 months, underscoring systemic vulnerability in crypto platforms' lack of distributed authorization controls compared to traditional finance.
